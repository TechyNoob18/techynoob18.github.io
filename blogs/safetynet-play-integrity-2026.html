The Ultimate Fix: How to Pass SafetyNet and Play Integrity on Any Rooted Phone (2026 Guide)

Rooting your Android phone opens up a world of possibilities, from custom ROMs and exclusive Pixel features to deep system optimization. But for years, there has been one major thorn in the side of the root community: SafetyNet and, more recently, Play Integrity.

It’s a frustrating cycle. You root your phone, everything is great, and then you try to open your banking app, Google Wallet, or even Pokemon GO, and—boom. Crash. "Device not certified." "Security violation."

Google is constantly updating its detection methods, making older fixes like "Universal SafetyNet Fix" obsolete. However, in 2026, the community has fought back with a new, robust combination of modules.

In a recent comprehensive guide on TechyNoob, we broke down exactly how to bypass these restrictions on any rooted device, whether you are using Magisk or KernelSU. Here is the complete, step-by-step tutorial to get those green checkmarks back.

The Problem: Why Does It Fail?

When you root your device, you are essentially unlocking the "admin" door to your operating system. While this is great for you, app developers (especially banks and game studios) see this as a security risk. They use Google's Play Integrity API to check if your device has been tampered with.

If the API reports "MEETS_BASIC_INTEGRITY" or "MEETS_DEVICE_INTEGRITY" as failing, secure apps will simply refuse to run.

The Solution: The "Golden Combo"

This isn't a single-click fix. To beat the 2026 detection standards, we need a specific cocktail of five items:

  • Zygisk Next Module: A modern replacement for the built-in Zygisk implementation in Magisk.
  • Tricky Store: A clever module designed to spoof the Keybox storage, tricking the system into thinking it has a valid hardware-backed security key.
  • Play Integrity Fork: An updated fork of the classic fix, specifically maintained to keep up with Google's latest ban waves.
  • Integrity Box: The heavy lifter that handles the fingerprint spoofing.
  • KSU Web APK: The manager interface for KernelSU users (and useful for managing Integrity Box).

Step-by-Step Installation

  1. Disable Built-in Zygisk: Open your Magisk app settings. If Zygisk is enabled, turn it off and reboot. We are replacing it with something better.
  2. Flash Zygisk Next: Install the Zygisk Next zip file as a module. Do not reboot yet.
  3. Flash the Rest: Install Tricky Store, Play Integrity Fork, and Integrity Box one by one.
  4. Reboot: Once all four modules are installed, restart your phone.
  5. The Final Trigger: Open the Integrity Box manager app (or use the Action Button in the module settings via a terminal). Look for an "Action" or "Update" button. This forces the module to download a fresh, valid certification fingerprint from the server.

Once clicked, run your favorite Play Integrity Checker app. You should now see the beautiful words: MEETS_BASIC_INTEGRITY and MEETS_DEVICE_INTEGRITY.

What Does This Mean for You?

With these checks passing:

  • Banking Apps: Your Chase, Wells Fargo, or local banking app will open without detecting root.
  • Google Wallet: Tap-to-pay (NFC) will function correctly.
  • Gaming: Games with strict anti-cheat (like PUBG or COD Mobile) will no longer ban you for having a rooted environment.
  • Streaming: Apps like Netflix will allow you to play content in HD/4K (Widevine L1).
