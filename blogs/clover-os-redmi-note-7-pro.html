Is Clover OS (Android 16) the Best ROM for Redmi Note 7 Pro in 2026? A Deep Dive

The Redmi Note 7 Pro is a legend. Released years ago, it redefined the budget smartphone market. But in the tech world, legends often fade into obsolescence as manufacturers stop sending updates. However, the custom ROM community has once again proven that hardware is only as dead as the software running on it.

In a recent comprehensive review on TechyNoob, we explored Clover OS, a custom ROM based on the bleeding-edge Android 16. Yes, you read that right. While modern mid-rangers are struggling to get Android 15, the humble Note 7 Pro is already tasting the future.

But is it actually usable as a daily driver? Does the battery hold up? And what about banking apps? Let’s break down everything you need to know.

The Android 16 Experience

The first thing you notice after booting up Clover OS is the smoothness. Seeing the Android 16 logo and Easter egg on a Snapdragon 675 processor feels surreal. The developers have done an incredible job porting the latest AOSP (Android Open Source Project) codebase to this aging hardware.

The interface is clean, modern, and surprisingly fluid. Animations are distinct and updated, lacking the stutter you might expect from running 2026 software on 2019 hardware.

Features That Matter

Clover OS isn't just a barebones port; it comes packed with "quality of life" features that stock Android often misses.

  • Privacy & Connectivity: One standout feature is the pre-configured DNS settings. You can switch to AdGuard DNS directly from the network menu to block ads system-wide without installing third-party apps.
  • Gaming Performance: We tested it with BGMI and COD Mobile. While you won't get 90 FPS on this hardware, the gameplay was stable at 60 FPS on low settings. The thermal management is excellent; the phone gets warm but never dangerously hot.
  • Audio: It comes with Moto Dolby Audio pre-installed. This is a massive win for media consumption, significantly boosting the volume and clarity of the single bottom-firing speaker.

The Battery King?

I used to swear by Superior OS when people asked for the best battery backup on this device. However, the crown has officially been passed. In our testing, Clover OS delivered an astonishing 7 hours and 15 minutes of Screen-on-Time (SOT). For a device with a battery this old, running the latest OS, that is phenomenal efficiency.

Benchmarks

It’s not all just battery life; the phone needs to perform. We ran standard CPU benchmarks to see how the Snapdragon 675 holds up in 2026.

  • Single Core: 647
  • Multi-Core: 1674

These are respectable numbers. They ensure that daily tasks—social media scrolling, messaging, and light multitasking—happen without major lag. It won't compete with a flagship, but it certainly doesn't feel like a budget phone from six years ago.

The "Gotchas": SafetyNet

No custom ROM is perfect, and Clover OS has one hurdle: SafetyNet. Out of the box, the ROM fails SafetyNet checks. This happens regardless of whether you are rooted or not. What this means is that banking apps and Google Wallet might not work immediately. The fix requires utilizing the built-in JSON injector in the settings or flashing a Magisk module to turn those checks green.

Conclusion

Clover OS breathes new life into the Redmi Note 7 Pro. It offers the sleekness of Android 16, incredible battery life that beats almost everything else in its class, and premium audio features baked right in. If you have a Note 7 Pro gathering dust, now is the time to unlock that bootloader and flash this ROM.
