Unlock Unlimited Google Photos Storage: The Revanced & Micro-G Method (No Root Required!)

Published on June 9, 2025 by TechyNoob

For years, Google Photos was the undisputed king of cloud photo storage, offering seemingly endless space for your cherished memories. However, the game changed when Google ended its free unlimited "High quality" storage policy in June 2021. Now, every new photo and video you upload counts towards your Google account's 15GB free storage, or your Google One subscription. This shift has left many users scrambling for solutions to avoid paying for extra space.

Fear not! There's a powerful open-source workaround that can bring back that coveted unlimited storage – and the best part is, you don't even need to root your Android device! In this comprehensive guide, we'll dive deep into how to achieve unlimited Google Photos storage using Revanced Google Photos v7.31 in conjunction with Micro-G.

Understanding the Google Photos Storage Dilemma

Before we jump into the solution, let's briefly recap why this method is so valuable. Google Photos became incredibly popular for its generous free storage, smart organization, and powerful AI features. However, as the volume of photos and videos uploaded globally exploded, maintaining truly unlimited storage became unsustainable for Google. The policy change in June 2021 meant that only photos uploaded before that date remained exempt from storage limits. New uploads, whether in "Storage saver" or "Original quality," now consume your shared Google account storage (across Gmail, Google Drive, and Photos).

This change hit users hard, especially those with large photo libraries or who frequently take high-resolution videos. While Google One offers paid storage plans, many users prefer a free solution, especially for a feature that was once standard.

Introducing the Game-Changers: Revanced Google Photos and Micro-G

Our solution hinges on two key open-source applications:

  • Revanced Google Photos (v7.31): This is a modified version of the official Google Photos application. The "Revanced" project aims to patch and enhance various Android apps, often by restoring features removed by developers, adding new functionalities, or removing restrictions. In the case of Google Photos, the specific patch in v7.31 (and similar versions) tricks Google's servers into believing your device is a Pixel phone from a generation that still qualifies for unlimited "High quality" (now "Storage saver") uploads. This "spoofing" is what unlocks the unlimited storage capability.
  • Micro-G: Often misunderstood, Micro-G is a free and open-source re-implementation of Google's proprietary user-space apps and libraries. Essentially, it's a lightweight, privacy-friendly alternative to Google Play Services. Many Android apps rely on Google Play Services for core functionalities like push notifications, location services, and Google account authentication. Since Revanced Google Photos is a modified app and might not fully play nice with official Google Play Services, Micro-G provides the necessary framework for it to authenticate with your Google account without Google's full suite of services. This is crucial for logging in and backing up your photos.

Together, these two applications create a powerful synergy that allows you to bypass the storage limitations imposed by Google on non-Pixel devices.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Get Unlimited Google Photos Storage

The process is straightforward, but it's vital to follow the steps precisely to ensure proper functionality.

Prerequisites:

  • An Android smartphone (no root required!).
  • A stable internet connection.
  • "Install from Unknown Sources" enabled in your phone's security settings (this allows you to install APK files downloaded outside the Google Play Store).

Download the Necessary APKs:

You will need to download the APK files for both Revanced Google Photos v7.31 and Micro-G. It is critically important to download these from trusted and reputable sources. Unofficial APKs can sometimes contain malware or be outdated. We recommend checking established Android communities, open-source project repositories, or well-known APK archives.

Installation Process:

Install Micro-G First:

  1. Locate the downloaded `Micro-G.apk` file on your device (usually in your "Downloads" folder).
  2. Tap on the file to begin the installation.
  3. If prompted, allow installation from "Unknown Sources."
  4. Complete the installation. Do NOT open Micro-G yet.

Install Revanced Google Photos v7.31:

  1. Next, find the downloaded `Revanced_Google_Photos_v7.31.apk` file.
  2. Tap on it to install.
  3. Again, grant any necessary permissions, including installation from unknown sources if prompted.
  4. Complete the installation. Do NOT open Revanced Google Photos yet.

Log In Through Micro-G:

  1. Now that both apps are installed, open the Micro-G application.
  2. Inside Micro-G, you will see an option to "Add account" or "Login with Google account." Tap on this.
  3. Proceed to log in with the Google account you wish to use for unlimited photo storage. This step is crucial as Micro-G handles the authentication for the Revanced app.
  4. Once you've successfully logged in, you can close the Micro-G app.

Open Revanced Google Photos:

  1. Finally, you can now open the Revanced Google Photos application.
  2. Upon opening, you will likely be prompted to select your Google account. This is a key limitation of the Revanced patch – you'll need to select your account each time you open the app. Choose the Google account you just logged into via Micro-G.
  3. Allow the app to access your photos and videos if prompted.

That's it! Your Revanced Google Photos app should now function with unlimited "Storage saver" quality backups, essentially behaving like a Pixel phone from an era that still enjoyed this perk.

Important Considerations and Limitations

While this method is incredibly effective for unlimited storage, it's essential to be aware of its limitations:

  • No AI Features like Magic Editor: The Revanced version of Google Photos is a patched application. It will not have access to the latest, advanced AI-powered editing features like Magic Editor, Photo Unblur, or other Pixel-exclusive computational photography tools that Google frequently rolls out. These features are tightly integrated with Google's cloud AI infrastructure and official app versions.
  • Account Selection Every Time: As mentioned, you will need to manually select your Google account every time you launch the Revanced Google Photos app. This is a minor inconvenience but necessary due to the nature of the patch.
  • Security and Trust: While Micro-G is an open-source project with a strong reputation for privacy, and the Revanced project is generally well-regarded in the modding community, installing APKs from outside official app stores always carries a minimal inherent risk. Always download from trusted sources.
  • Updates: Updates for Revanced Google Photos are not automatic like official apps. You'll need to manually check for new versions and install them if you want to stay updated. Micro-G also receives updates.
  • Google's Policy Changes: While this method works now, Google could implement new measures in the future that might block or detect this spoofing, rendering the method ineffective. As of June 2025, it remains a viable solution.

Why This Method is a Game-Changer

Despite the minor limitations, this Revanced + Micro-G combination offers a significant advantage for users who:

  • Have extensive photo and video libraries and want to avoid cloud storage subscription fees.
  • Prefer to keep their memories backed up to Google Photos but don't own a Pixel phone that qualifies for unlimited storage.
  • Are privacy-conscious and appreciate Micro-G's open-source nature compared to full Google Play Services.
  • Want to maximize their phone's local storage by moving photos to the cloud without constraint.
