Get Google Photos Unlimited Storage (WITHOUT ROOT!)

Published on June 9, 2025 by TechyNoob

Remember the golden days of Google Photos offering unlimited high-quality storage? While those days are officially over for most, what if we told you there's still a way to enjoy unlimited Google Photos cloud storage on your Android device in 2025, and that too, without root access? Yes, you heard that right!

In this comprehensive guide, we'll walk you through a clever method using Revanced and Micro-G to trick Google Photos into believing your device is an eligible Pixel phone, thus unlocking unlimited storage.

Understanding the Trick: Pixel Spoofing

Google offers unlimited "original quality" photo and video backups exclusively to its Pixel line of smartphones. This trick leverages a modification that makes your non-Pixel Android device appear as a Pixel device to Google Photos, enabling the unlimited backup feature.

The primary tools we'll be using are:

  • Revanced Manager: An unofficial patching tool for Android apps, allowing you to modify and enhance existing APKs. We'll use it to patch the Google Photos app.
  • Micro-G: A free and open-source re-implementation of Google's proprietary user-space apps and libraries. It's essential for ensuring patched Google Photos works without full Google Play Services.

Prerequisites: What You Need Before You Start

Before you begin, ensure you have the following ready:

  • Android Device: A non-rooted Android smartphone.
  • Stable Internet Connection: For downloading apps and patches.
  • Sufficient Storage: Ensure you have enough free storage on your device for app downloads and patched APKs.
  • Enable Unknown Sources: Go to your phone's Security settings and enable "Install unknown apps" or "Unknown sources" for your browser or file manager.
  • Original Google Photos App: Ensure you have the original Google Photos app installed (or download it from Play Store if you don't).
  • Patience: This process involves several steps, but it's worth it!

Step-by-Step Guide: Unlimited Google Photos Storage

Follow these steps carefully.

Phase 1: Prepare Your Device and Install Micro-G

  1. Uninstall Official Google Photos Updates: Go to Settings > Apps > Google Photos. Tap on the three dots (or equivalent menu) and select "Uninstall updates." This will revert the app to its factory version.
  2. Uninstall Micro-G (if previously installed): If you have any older version of Micro-G, uninstall it completely.
  3. Download and Install Micro-G: Download the latest stable Micro-G APK from its official source (microg.org). Install the APK.

Phase 2: Patch Google Photos using Revanced Manager

  1. Download and Install Revanced Manager: Download the latest Revanced Manager APK from its official GitHub repository (github.com/revanced/revanced-manager). Install the APK.
  2. Open Revanced Manager: Launch the Revanced Manager app.
  3. Select `Patch` and `Applications`: In Revanced Manager, go to the `Patch` tab and select `Applications`.
  4. Select Google Photos: Find and select "Google Photos" from the list of installed applications.
  5. Select Patches:
    • Scroll down and select the "Spoof Google Photos" patch.
    • Also, select the "Micro-G support" patch.
    • You may also choose other patches if you wish (e.g., ad-blocking, layout tweaks).
  6. Start Patching: Tap on the "Patch" button (usually a blue or purple button at the bottom). The patching process will begin and may take several minutes.
  7. Install Patched APK: Once patching is complete, Revanced Manager will prompt you to "Install" the new patched Google Photos APK. Tap install. Allow installation from unknown sources if prompted.
  8. Do NOT Open Google Photos yet.

Phase 3: Final Configuration and Verification

  1. Grant Permissions: Go to Settings > Apps > Google Photos (the newly patched one). Grant all necessary permissions, especially "Photos and videos" or "Storage."
  2. Clear Cache and Data for Google Photos (Optional but Recommended): Go to Settings > Apps > Google Photos > Storage & cache. Clear both cache and storage.
  3. Add Google Account to Micro-G: Open the Micro-G app. Go to "Accounts" and add your Google Account. This is crucial for Google Photos to function correctly.
  4. Open Google Photos: Now, open the patched Google Photos app.
  5. Verify Unlimited Backup: Go to Google Photos Settings > Backup. You should now see an option indicating "Unlimited storage from this device" or a similar message that signifies Pixel spoofing is successful. You can start backing up your photos and videos.

Important Notes & Troubleshooting:

  • Updates: Google Photos updates frequently. If an update breaks the unlimited storage, you will need to uninstall the patched Google Photos, uninstall its updates (if it automatically updated), and repeat the patching process with the latest compatible versions of Revanced Manager and Micro-G.
  • Micro-G for Services: Micro-G replaces core Google Play Services functionalities required by Google Photos. Ensure it's running in the background and has all necessary permissions.
  • Original Quality Only: This trick enables unlimited *original quality* storage, similar to how Pixel devices work. Make sure your backup settings in Google Photos are set to "Original quality."
  • SafetyNet: This method does NOT require root and generally doesn't affect SafetyNet/Play Integrity status.
  • Source Reliability: Always download Revanced Manager and Micro-G from their official GitHub repositories or trusted sources to avoid malicious software.
