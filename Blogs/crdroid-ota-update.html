Keeping Your Device Fresh: A Comprehensive Guide to Installing CrDroid ROM OTA Updates via Local Method

Published on June 12, 2025 by TechyNoob

CrDroid has emerged as a popular choice for Android enthusiasts seeking a feature-rich, stable, and highly customizable aftermarket firmware. Based on the latest Android Open Source Project (AOSP), CrDroid offers a blend of performance enhancements, unique features, and a clean user experience. Just like any operating system, CrDroid receives regular updates to improve stability, introduce new features, and patch security vulnerabilities. Keeping your CrDroid ROM up-to-date is crucial for optimal performance and security.

While there are a couple of ways to update your CrDroid ROM, including the more technical ADB sideloading method, the local OTA (Over-The-Air) update option provides a user-friendly and straightforward approach for most users. This method allows you to update your device without needing to connect to a computer or use complex commands.

In this comprehensive guide, we'll walk you through the simple steps of installing CrDroid ROM OTA updates using the local method. While we'll primarily reference the process on the OnePlus 6 and OnePlus 6T, the beauty of this method lies in its general applicability to each and every phone which has CrDroid custom ROM installed. So, regardless of the device you're enjoying CrDroid on, this article will provide you with the knowledge to keep it running smoothly with the latest updates.

Understanding the Update Methods: A Quick Overview

Before we dive into the local OTA method, let's briefly touch upon the two primary ways to update your CrDroid ROM:

  • ADB Sideloading: This method involves connecting your phone to a computer and using the Android Debug Bridge (ADB) tool to manually push the update zip file to your device for installation via recovery mode. This method is often used when there are issues with other update methods or when a clean flash is recommended alongside the update.
  • Local OTA Update: This is the method we'll be focusing on in this article. It allows you to download the update zip file directly to your phone and install it through the phone's settings without requiring a computer connection for the installation process itself. This is generally the easier and more convenient option for most users.

Focusing on Simplicity: Installing CrDroid OTA via Local Update

The local OTA update method is designed for ease of use. Hereâ€™s a step-by-step guide to help you install the latest CrDroid updates on your device:

Step 1: Download the OTA Zip File

The first and most crucial step is to download the correct OTA zip file for your specific device and the version of CrDroid you are currently running. These files are typically provided by the CrDroid development team. Make sure you download the OTA update package and not a full ROM flash file, unless explicitly instructed otherwise.

Important Note: Always download the update file from a trusted source, such as the official CrDroid website or recognized community forums.

Once the download is complete, it's highly recommended to keep the downloaded OTA zip file in the internal storage of your phone. For easy access, placing it in the root directory (the main storage area) is usually the most convenient approach.

Step 2: Navigate to the Update Section in Settings

Next, you need to access the system update settings on your phone. To do this:

  1. Open the Settings app on your device.
  2. Scroll through the settings menu until you find a section labeled "System" or "About phone." The exact wording might vary slightly depending on your device and CrDroid version.
  3. Within the "System" or "About phone" section, look for an option related to "Updates" or "System updates." Tap on this option to proceed.

Step 3: Access the Local Update Menu

This is where you'll initiate the local update process:

  1. On the System update screen, look for a three-dot menu icon. This is usually located in the top right corner of the screen.
  2. Tap on the three-dot menu icon. A small menu with several options should appear.
  3. From the menu, look for an option like "Local update," "Choose local update package," or "Select local update file." The specific wording can differ slightly based on the CrDroid version you have installed. Tap on this option.

Step 4: Select the OTA Zip File

Tapping on the local update option will open your phone's built-in file manager. Now, you need to locate the CrDroid OTA zip file that you downloaded in Step 1.

  1. Navigate through the file manager to your phone's internal storage.
  2. Find the OTA zip file you downloaded. If you followed our recommendation, it should be in the root directory for easy access.
  3. Once you locate the file, tap on it to select it.

Step 5: Confirm and Install the Update

After selecting the OTA zip file, your phone will typically ask you to confirm that you want to install the selected update package. Carefully review the confirmation message to ensure you've selected the correct file.

Once you are sure, confirm the installation process. Your phone will then begin the update process. This usually involves verifying the update package and then proceeding with the installation.

Important Note: The installation process might take a few minutes, and your phone will likely restart automatically during the installation. This is perfectly normal, so don't be alarmed. Avoid interrupting the process by pressing any buttons or turning off your device.

Step 6: Reboot and Enjoy the Updated CrDroid ROM

Once the installation is complete, your phone will automatically reboot. After the reboot is finished, your device will be running the latest version of your CrDroid ROM! You can then explore the new features, enjoy the improvements, and experience the enhanced stability that comes with the update.
