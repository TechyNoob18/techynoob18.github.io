Android 16 Arrives: A New Era for Pixel Devices and Beyond

Published on June 10, 2025 by TechyNoob

Today marks a significant milestone in the Android ecosystem as Google officially rolls out Android 16 to the Pixel 6 and all later Pixel devices. This release signals a strategic shift from Google, decoupling the platform update from specific Pixel hardware launches, a decision that promises more breathing room for both software and hardware development. While Pixel users are the first to experience the stable build, other Android OEMs are expected to follow suit with their stable platform updates in the very near future.

Google has also hinted at the future with Android 16 QPR1 betas already in circulation for Pixel devices, though the features within these betas are slated for a broader Android platform release with Android 17.

Let's delve into what we know about Android 16, from its naming convention to a comprehensive look at its confirmed features.

Android 16: The Name Game

In a move that solidifies a trend initiated with Android 10, Android 16 sheds the traditional dessert codename, opting for the straightforward "Android 16." This continues Google's commitment to simpler, version-number-based branding for public releases.

However, the internal world at Google still cherishes its sweet traditions. Android 16's internal codename has been revealed as "Baklava." This choice is particularly intriguing, as it breaks the alphabetical progression seen in previous internal codenames (Android 15 was "Vanilla Ice Cream"). This jump from "V" to "B" is attributed to significant backend changes within Android's core architecture.

Android 16: A Preponed Release

Google had previously indicated an earlier-than-usual release for Android 16, and today's rollout confirms this. The new timeline saw Android 16 reach Platform Stability with its Beta 3 release in March 2025, followed by a second Platform Stability release with Beta 4 in April 2025, culminating in today's Stable Release on June 10, 2025.

This accelerated schedule represents a notable departure from Android's historical release cadence, which typically saw major versions arriving in Q3 or Q4. Google's rationale for this shift is to "better align with the schedule of device launches across [its] ecosystem, so more devices can get the major release of Android sooner." This could mean future Pixel 10 series, launch with the very latest Android version out of the box.

Looking ahead, Google also plans more minor updates to the Android OS, with a minor release for Android 16 anticipated in Q4 2025, potentially as Android 16 QPR2.

For Pixel 6 and later Pixel devices, the Android 16 Update will arrive as an Over-The-Air (OTA) update. Beyond Pixels, some Android OEMs, including OnePlus and OPPO, have already offered Android 16-based developer betas for select devices, and Samsung is expected to deliver its stable Android 16 update this summer.

Android 16: The Easter Egg

A beloved Android tradition, the Android Easter Egg, makes its return in Android 16. Continuing the theme from Android 14 and 15, Android 16 features a spaceship minigame that encourages users to explore the vastness of space.

To discover this hidden gem, navigate to Settings > About phone and repeatedly tap on the Android version until the Android 16 logo appears floating in space. A long press on this logo will launch the spaceship minigame.

Android 16's Easter Egg introduces new functionality. Users can now manually engage the autopilot system within the game via a new "AUTO" button in the bottom right-hand corner. With autopilot engaged, the Easter Egg displays a progress-centric notification, showcasing a key part of the new Live Updates feature in Android 16. While the full Live Updates experience isn't present in the stable release, this notification provides a glimpse into its capabilities.

Android 16: Confirmed Features and Changes

Android 16 is packed with a multitude of New Features and improvements, enhancing user experience, privacy, security, and developer capabilities. Here's a rundown of the confirmed highlights:

  • Live Updates: A new class of notifications designed to monitor and provide prominent access to important ongoing activities like ride-sharing, food delivery, and navigation. In the initial stable release, this manifests as progress-centric notifications that display more information and a progress tracker within the notification itself. Future updates are expected to bring a Live Update chip in the status bar and consistent display on the lock screen and Always On Display.
  • Force Group Notifications: Android 16 now automatically groups notifications from the same app, bundling all alerts from a single application together for a more organized notification experience.
  • Notification Cooldown: This feature, previously a Pixel exclusive, arrives for the wider Android platform. It gradually lowers the alert volume for up to a minute when you receive a burst of notifications from the same app.
  • Power Button Shortcut for Wallet Apps: Users can now customize a double-press of the power button to open Google Wallet, offering a quick alternative to the camera shortcut.
  • HDR Screenshots: True HDR screenshots are now supported, saving HDR content as PNG files with embedded HDR gainmaps for vibrant viewing on Android 16 devices and SDR on others.
  • New Emojis from Unicode 16.0: Android 16 incorporates an updated emoji font file, introducing seven new emojis, including "Face with Bags Under Eyes," "Fingerprint," and "Shovel."
  • Embedded Photo Picker: New APIs grant app developers more control over the appearance of the photo picker while maintaining security and privacy. Future updates will enable searching from cloud media providers within the photo picker.
  • Health Records Integration: Health Connect now supports Electronic Health Records (EHRs) in the FHIR standard, allowing reading and writing of sensitive health data, though this feature is currently limited to an early access program.
  • Better Adaptive Refresh Rate (ARR) Support: New APIs facilitate easier adoption of Adaptive Refresh Rate for app developers, promising improved support for dynamic display refresh rates.
  • Better App Adaptability: Android 16 discourages apps from restricting screen orientation and resizability on large-screen devices, with a mandatory shift expected in Android 17.
  • APV Codec Support: Support for the Advanced Professional Video (APV) 422-10 Profile is added, promising perceptually lossless video quality with reduced storage requirements.
  • Predictive Back Upgrades: Enhanced APIs enable predictive back system animations in gesture navigation, and for three-button navigation, a long-press of the back button now initiates a predictive back animation.
  • Privacy Sandbox on Android: The latest version of Privacy Sandbox on Android is integrated.
  • WritingTools API to Let Apps Block AI Writing Tools: This API allows apps to optionally disable AI rewriting text where irrelevant, such as for passwords or number input.
  • Generic Ranging APIs: The new RangingManager provides ways to determine the distance and angle between devices using technologies like BLE channel sounding, Ultra-Wideband, and Wi-Fi round-trip time, potentially leading to better device tracker support.
  • Night Mode Camera Extension API: A new API assists apps in automatically adjusting to low-light environments for image and video capture.
  • Vertical Text Support: Low-level support for rendering and measuring text vertically is introduced, beneficial for languages like Japanese.
  • App Functions API: Gemini Extensions: This API allows developers to expose their app's key features to the phone's default assistant, enabling Gemini to execute functions directly within apps.
  • Measurement System Customization: Users can now set their preferred measurement system independently of the system language.
  • Camera Updates: New hybrid auto-exposure modes are added to the Camera2 API, along with support for fine color temperature and tint adjustments for professional video recording.
  • Media Updates: A standard Intent action for capturing and displaying Motion Photos is introduced, alongside support for Ultra HDR images in the HEIC format and future AVIF support.
  • Mandating Edge-to-Edge Apps: The transitional API allowing apps to opt out of edge-to-edge display has been deprecated, making it mandatory for apps targeting Android 16 to display content edge-to-edge.
  • Advanced Protection Mode: This new mode integrates Google's Advanced Protection Program into Android, offering a one-click solution to bolster phone security against various threats. It activates features like Intrusion Logging, Theft Detection Lock, and enhanced Google Play Protect.
  • Automatically Hiding Sensitive Notifications from Lock Screen: Android 16 extends security by redacting sensitive notifications (like OTP codes) before displaying them on the lock screen in higher-risk scenarios.
  • Anti-Scammer Safeguards During Phone Calls: Android 16 prevents users from enabling sideloading permission and certain sensitive permissions (like accessibility access) during active phone calls, combating scam attempts.
  • Ambient Volume Controls for Bluetooth LE Audio Hearing Aids: Users of Bluetooth LE Audio hearing aids can now adjust the volume of ambient sound picked up by their hearing aids' microphones.
  • Auracast Broadcast Audio Support: Official support for Auracast broadcast audio is introduced with compatible LE Audio hearing aids on Pixel 9 devices, enabling direct audio streams from public venues.
  • Outline Text for Maximum Text Contrast: A new outline text feature replaces high-contrast text, drawing larger contrasting areas around text to significantly improve legibility.
  • Vulkan, the Official Graphics API for Android: Google has declared Vulkan as the official graphics API for Android, aiming to establish it as the GPU hardware abstraction layer. This will lead to more immersive visuals in games and improved performance.
  • iOS-like App Settings with SettingsPreferenceService API: A new API allows apps to integrate their settings directly into the Android Settings app, potentially creating a unified hub for device and app configurations.
  • In-Process Software Audio Codecs: Android 16 adds support for in-process software audio codecs, allowing media codecs to run within an app's process.

Conclusion: A Powerful Leap Forward

Android 16 represents a comprehensive update, bringing a mix of user-facing enhancements, significant under-the-hood improvements, and bolstered security measures. Pixel users can now explore these new functionalities, with the anticipation that these advancements will soon reach a wider array of Android devices.
