Prerequisites for a Smooth Installation

Before we dive into the installation steps, ensure your Android phone meets these essential requirements. This process relies heavily on Magisk, the popular systemless rooting solution.

  • Rooted Android Phone: Your device must be rooted with Magisk. If your phone isn't rooted yet, you'll need to follow a separate guide to achieve this first. Magisk allows for system-level modifications without directly altering the /system partition, making it safer and easier to manage modules.
  • Magisk Manager App: You need the Magisk Manager application installed and fully functional on your device. This app is your gateway to managing all Magisk modules, including our boot animation files.
  • Downloaded Module Files: You'll need to download the specific boot animation Magisk module (.zip file) you wish to install. The download links will be provided in the section below.
  • Basic File Management Knowledge: Knowing how to navigate your phone's internal storage and locate downloaded files is essential.
  • A Backup (Highly Recommended): While Magisk modules are generally safe, it's always good practice to have a recent backup of your device's ROM (via TWRP custom recovery, for instance) before making system-level changes. This provides a safety net in case anything goes wrong, allowing you to easily revert.

Step-by-Step Installation Guide (with Magisk)

The beauty of using Magisk modules is their simplicity. Just follow these straightforward steps:

  1. Download Your Chosen Module: First, visit the download links provided in the next section and download the .zip file for the boot animation you want (Harmony OS, Android Robot, or Goku). Save it to an easily accessible location on your phone's internal storage, typically the "Downloads" folder.
  2. Open Magisk Manager: Locate and launch the Magisk Manager application from your app drawer. This is your control panel for all things Magisk.
  3. Navigate to the Modules Section: Once inside Magisk Manager, look for the navigation bar usually located at the bottom of the screen. Tap on the "Modules" icon. It often resembles a jigsaw puzzle piece. This section lists all your currently installed Magisk modules and allows you to add new ones.
  4. Initiate "Install from Storage": In the Modules section, you'll see an option like "Install from storage" or a similar button (sometimes represented by a "+" icon). Tap on this. This action will open your phone's built-in file browser.
  5. Locate and Select the Module File: Now, browse through your phone's storage to find the downloaded boot animation .zip file. As mentioned, it's most likely in your "Downloads" folder. Tap on the .zip file you wish to flash.
  6. Magisk Flashing Process: Magisk Manager will now begin the flashing process. You'll see a log of commands and progress indicators scrolling on your screen. This typically takes only a few seconds. Wait patiently for the process to complete. You should see a message indicating "Done" or "Installation Successful."
  7. Reboot Your Phone: Once Magisk has successfully flashed the module, you'll be prompted with an option to "Reboot." Tap on this button immediately. A reboot is crucial for the newly installed boot animation to take effect and be displayed during the startup sequence.
  8. Enjoy Your New Boot Animation! As your phone restarts, instead of your old boot screen, you should now be greeted by the stunning new custom boot animation you just installed! Congratulations, you've successfully personalized your Android's startup experience.

Important Considerations & Troubleshooting

While this process is generally smooth, here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • ROM Compatibility: The most common reason for a boot animation not changing after installation is ROM incompatibility. Some custom ROMs, especially those with heavily modified system UIs or specific proprietary boot animation formats (like Samsung's .qmg files on One UI devices), might not fully support external boot animation changes via the standard Android mechanism that Magisk modules utilize.What to do: If your boot animation doesn't change, check your custom ROM's official XDA Developers thread or community forums. Look for discussions regarding boot animation support or alternative methods specific to that ROM.
  • Multiple Boot Animation Modules: Avoid installing multiple boot animation modules simultaneously. This can lead to conflicts or prevent any of them from working correctly. If you want to switch, either disable or uninstall the existing boot animation module in Magisk Manager before installing a new one.
  • Module Uninstallation/Disabling: If you ever want to revert to your stock boot animation or try a different custom one, simply go back to the "Modules" section in Magisk Manager. You can then toggle off (disable) the installed boot animation module or choose to uninstall it completely. After disabling/uninstalling, reboot your phone for the changes to take effect.
  • Bootloop (Rare but Possible): In very rare cases, an incompatible module could cause a bootloop. This is why a custom recovery backup is crucial. If you get stuck in a bootloop, you can usually disable Magisk modules by renaming the magisk.zip file in TWRP, or by simply reflashing your ROM.
